Communication guide for NJSI

With members across New Jersey and internationally, clear and consistent communication is critical. Use this guide to stay aligned.

📢
Announcements
Use the Announcements panel for anything the entire team needs to see — events, deadlines, updates. Tag posts as Urgent, Event, or Reminder so members know the priority at a glance.
✓
Tasks
Every piece of work should be a task with a clear owner, due date, and priority. No more "did anyone do this?" — if it's not a task, it doesn't exist. Check off tasks when done to keep the dashboard accurate.
📅
Calendar
All meetings and deadlines go on the shared calendar. Include Zoom links for every virtual meeting. International members: always post times in EST and note it — members can convert to their own timezone.
⚡
Urgent matters
Tag announcements as Urgent when action is needed within 24 hours. Do not overuse this — if everything is urgent, nothing is. Reserve for time-sensitive decisions or emergencies only.
🌍
International members
Default meeting timezone is EST. Aim to schedule calls between 2–6 PM EST when possible (overlaps with European evening and African afternoon hours). Always send a meeting recap to those who can't attend live.
📋
Weekly rhythm
Monday: review your tasks for the week. Wednesday: post a mid-week update if you're blocked. Friday: mark completed tasks done and flag anything at risk. Consistency keeps the whole team on track.
Golden rules
1. If it's not written down (task or announcement), assume it didn't happen.
2. Assign every task to a specific person — "we'll handle it" means no one handles it.
3. If you're running late on a task, flag it early — not the day it's due.
4. Respect timezone differences. Not everyone is in NJ — plan with the whole team in mind.
5. Keep announcements concise and action-oriented. State what people need to do and by when.
